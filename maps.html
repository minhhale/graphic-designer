Transforming Complex Data into Engaging Maps

I create maps that transform complex geographic data into clear, visually engaging stories. Using tools like Illustrator, Mapbox, and GIS platforms, I blend precise data visualization with thoughtful color palettes, clean typography, and layered details to guide viewers through key insights while keeping the design accessible and compelling.

East Asia Trade Map

Overview:

This map highlights key Asia–Pacific trade partners, showing links across Asia, Oceania, and the United States for a Radio Free Asia report.

Design Goals:

Create a clean, neutral map that quickly orients viewers, uses subtle color to mark trade participants, and stays legible across web and print.

Process:
  • • Built in Illustrator with a Robinson projection for balanced geography.
  • • Applied soft blue oceans and light yellow highlights for clarity and professionalism.
  • • Used precise leader lines, consistent typography, and a global inset to show U.S. and Russia connections.
Result:

A concise regional snapshot that makes complex trade networks easy to read and visually polished.

Hong Kong & Mainland China Map

Overview:

This map details Hong Kong’s geography and its proximity to mainland China for a Radio Free Asia report, helping audiences visualize key districts and travel connections.

Design Goals:

Provide a clear regional snapshot that distinguishes Hong Kong from neighboring Shenzhen, while highlighting major areas such as Kowloon, Lantau Island, and the international airport.

Process:
  • • Created in Adobe Illustrator with clean vector outlines for easy scaling.
  • • Used soft earth tones to separate Hong Kong from mainland China and a light blue ocean for contrast.
  • • Added a locator inset showing Hong Kong’s position relative to Beijing to give global context.
  • • Applied consistent typography and leader lines for legible island and district names.
Result:

A concise, readable map that situates Hong Kong within China and underscores its key transport and territorial divisions, supporting news coverage on political and economic issues.

Armed Groups in Southern Philippines Map

Overview:

This map visualizes the distribution of armed groups across Mindanao to help readers understand the region’s complex security landscape. Created for a news analysis, it highlights the overlapping presence of insurgent and militant organizations in different provinces.

Design Goals
  • • Clearly delineate Mindanao’s sub-regions while showing where each armed group operates.
  • • Present detailed geographic data in a way that remains legible and balanced for both web and print.
  • • Provide quick reference to the groups’ names without overwhelming the map.
Process:
  • • Built in Adobe Illustrator using a Lambert Conformal Conic projection for accurate regional shape.
  • • Applied soft, distinct colors to differentiate each armed group while using light gray for provincial boundaries.
  • • Used leader lines and consistent typography to label groups and provinces clearly.
  • • Included a locator inset showing Mindanao’s position within the Philippines for context.
Result:

A clear, informative map that helps readers quickly grasp the complex presence of armed groups in Mindanao, supporting deeper understanding of the region’s security challenges.
