Interactive Maps & Charts for Impactful Stories

I design interactive maps and charts that transform complex data into engaging, story-driven visuals. Using tools like Mapbox, D3.js, and Flourish, I combine precise data visualization with intuitive interactions to invite exploration and support journalistic storytelling and social impact.

Myanmar Upper & Lower House Interactive Charts

Project Overview

These paired interactive charts track Myanmar’s Upper House (Amyothar Hluttaw) and Lower House (Pyithu Hluttaw) election results across four key contests - 2010, 2012 by-election, 2015, and 2020. Each seat is represented as a dot in a semicircle chamber layout, allowing viewers to see how party representation and the military’s guaranteed seats have shifted over time.

Design Goals
  • • Clear Comparisons: Show the changing balance between elected parties and the constitutionally reserved military seats.
  • • Historical Context: Enable easy toggling between years to reveal democratic gains and setbacks.
  • • Intuitive Interaction: Use hover states to provide party names and seat counts without cluttering the visual.
  • • Accessible Aesthetics: Choose color palettes and layouts that are visually appealing and easy to interpret.
  • • Engaging Storytelling: Highlight the 25% of seats controlled by the Armed Forces to emphasize the limits of Myanmar’s democratic reforms.
Process & Tools

I built both visualizations using Flourish, customizing its parliament chart template to match the exact seat counts of each chamber. Color palettes and hover interactions were fine-tuned to distinguish parties while keeping the interface clean and accessible. Supporting assets were refined in Illustrator to ensure consistent typography and responsive design.

Outcome

The final graphics give journalists and audiences an intuitive way to explore Myanmar’s parliamentary composition. By animating seat changes and spotlighting the military bloc (56 seats in the Upper House, 110 in the Lower House), the charts reveal how electoral victories still coexist with entrenched military power—an essential insight for understanding the country’s fragile democracy.

Myanmar State Election Interactive Map

Project Overview

This interactive map presents key election data across Myanmar’s states and regions, allowing users to explore local voting patterns and candidate information. Hovering over each state reveals voter counts, candidate totals, and population figures, turning raw statistics into an intuitive geographic story.

Design Goals
  • • Geographic Clarity: Provide a clear state-by-state view of Myanmar’s electoral landscape.
  • • Data Transparency: Surface crucial metrics—voters, candidates, population—on hover for quick reference.
  • • User Engagement: Encourage exploration by making each region clickable and responsive.
  • Visual Harmony: Use a cohesive color scheme that differentiates states while maintaining overall aesthetic balance.
  • • Contextual Insight: Highlight regional disparities and electoral dynamics to support deeper understanding of Myanmar’s political environment.
Process & Tools

Built with Flourish, this map uses a custom color palette to differentiate each state while maintaining visual harmony and accessibility. I fine-tuned the tooltip design for clarity and added smooth hover transitions to keep interactions seamless. Supporting layout and typography were refined in Illustrator to ensure consistency across the election graphics suite.

Outcome

The final piece gives journalists and audiences an at-a-glance understanding of Myanmar’s regional voting dynamics. By combining geographic context with live data, it supports reporting and civic engagement, highlighting the diversity and scale of participation in the country’s elections.
